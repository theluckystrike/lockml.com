About LockML

Our mission to close the ML security gap and the network of resources we contribute to.

Our Mission

LockML exists because the gap between ML adoption and ML security is dangerously wide. Organizations are deploying machine learning systems into critical infrastructure, healthcare, finance, and autonomous systems at an accelerating pace, but security practices have not kept up. Most ML teams have no formal security training specific to machine learning, no adversarial testing in their development pipeline, and no monitoring capable of detecting ML-specific attacks in production. LockML's mission is to close this gap by making ML security knowledge practical, accessible, and actionable.

We focus on translating academic ML security research into practical engineering guidance. The adversarial ML research community produces excellent work, but most of it is published in formats that working engineers do not have time to read or know how to implement. Our checklists, frameworks, and guides bridge this gap by distilling the most important findings into step-by-step implementation guidance with code examples, architecture diagrams, and clear success criteria.

We believe that ML security is not a niche specialty but a core competency that every ML team needs. Just as the software industry learned that security cannot be an afterthought bolted on at the end of development, the ML industry needs to learn the same lesson before a major security incident forces the issue. Our goal is to accelerate that learning curve and help teams build security into their ML practices from the ground up, before an incident makes it urgent.

Part of a Larger Network

LockML is part of a network of specialized AI and machine learning resources. Our flagship resource, zovo.one, provides a comprehensive hub for discovering AI tools and platforms with security considerations integrated into the evaluation criteria. LockML contributes the security-specific perspective to this network, ensuring that teams can access deep expertise in ML threat modeling, adversarial defense, and compliance regardless of where they are in their security maturity journey.
